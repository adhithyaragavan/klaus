GOODS AND EQUIPMENT SUPPLY AGREEMENT

This Goods and Equipment Supply Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of June 1, 2025, by
and between Klaus Compliance Solutions, Inc. ("Company") and Wayne Industrial Supply Co.
("Vendor"), for the supply of office and data center hardware.

Section 1. Definitions

1.1 "Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by either Party in
connection with this Agreement, including pricing terms, purchase forecasts, and Company's
facility specifications.

1.2 "Order" means a purchase order issued by Company and accepted by Vendor under this
Agreement.

Section 2. Confidentiality

2.1 Each Party shall protect the other Party's Confidential Information with the same degree of
care it uses for its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care.

2.2 Neither Party shall disclose the pricing terms of this Agreement to any third party without
the other Party's prior written consent.

2.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations under this Section 2 shall survive termination of
this Agreement for a period of one (1) year.

Section 3. Data Handling and Security

3.1 Vendor's access to Company data under this Agreement is limited to invoicing and shipping
information necessary to fulfill Orders.

3.2 Vendor shall not use Company's invoicing or shipping data for any purpose other than
fulfilling Orders and shall not sell or share such data with third parties.

3.3 Breach Notification. Vendor shall notify Company within ten (10) business days of
discovering any unauthorized access to Company's invoicing or shipping data held by Vendor.

Section 4. Limitation of Liability

4.1 Each Party's aggregate liability arising out of this Agreement shall not exceed three
hundred thousand dollars ($300,000).

4.2 Vendor's liability for defective goods is additionally limited to repair, replacement, or
refund of the purchase price of the affected goods, at Vendor's option.

Section 5. Term and Termination

5.1 This Agreement remains in effect for twenty-four (24) months from the effective date.

5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon forty-five (45) days' prior
written notice.

5.3 Company may terminate immediately if Vendor fails to fulfill three (3) or more Orders within
their agreed delivery windows in any rolling six (6) month period.

Section 6. General Provisions

6.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New Jersey.

6.2 Force Majeure. Neither Party is liable for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable
control, including natural disasters, labor disputes, and supply chain disruptions.
