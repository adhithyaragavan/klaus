DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM AND SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Data Processing Addendum and Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
February 10, 2025, by and between Klaus Compliance Solutions, Inc. ("Company," acting as
data controller) and Initech Data Solutions, Inc. ("Vendor," acting as data processor), for the
provision of payroll data processing services.

Section 1. Definitions

1.1 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural
person processed by Vendor on behalf of Company, including employee payroll records, bank
account details, and tax identification numbers.

1.2 "Subprocessor" means any third party engaged by Vendor to process Personal Data on
Vendor's behalf.

1.3 "Security Incident" means any confirmed or reasonably suspected unauthorized access to,
acquisition of, or disclosure of Personal Data.

Section 2. Confidentiality

2.1 Vendor shall treat all Personal Data and Company business information as strictly
confidential and shall not disclose it to any party other than an approved Subprocessor bound
by written confidentiality obligations equivalent to those in this Agreement.

2.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by
confidentiality obligations and have received data protection training.

2.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations in this Section 2 shall survive termination of
this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, reflecting the sensitivity of the payroll data
processed under this Agreement.

Section 3. Data Handling and Security

3.1 Vendor shall process Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing the Services and
strictly in accordance with Company's documented instructions.

3.2 Vendor shall encrypt Personal Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent, and in transit using
TLS 1.2 or higher.

3.3 Vendor shall not engage any Subprocessor without Company's prior written approval, and shall
maintain a current list of approved Subprocessors available to Company upon request.

3.4 Breach Notification. Vendor shall notify Company of any Security Incident within
twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of it, and shall provide a written incident report
within five (5) business days detailing the scope, cause, and remediation steps, given the
heightened sensitivity of payroll and financial data processed under this Agreement.

Section 4. Limitation of Liability

4.1 Each Party's aggregate liability arising out of this Agreement shall not exceed two million
dollars ($2,000,000), except that this cap does not apply to Vendor's liability for a Security
Incident caused by Vendor's failure to implement the safeguards required under Section 3.

4.2 Vendor shall indemnify Company for reasonable costs incurred in responding to a Security
Incident caused by Vendor's breach of this Agreement, including notification costs and
regulatory fines directly attributable to Vendor's non-compliance.

Section 5. Term and Termination

5.1 This Agreement remains in effect for an initial term of eighteen (18) months.

5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for material breach upon written notice, provided
the breaching Party fails to cure the breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving such notice.

5.3 Upon termination, Vendor shall return or securely delete all Personal Data within thirty
(30) days, and shall certify such deletion in writing upon Company's request.

Section 6. General Provisions

6.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Illinois.

6.2 Audit Rights. Company may audit Vendor's data processing practices, including those of any
approved Subprocessor, upon reasonable prior notice, no more than once per calendar year absent
a suspected Security Incident.
