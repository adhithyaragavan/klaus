CLOUD HOSTING AND INFRASTRUCTURE SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Cloud Hosting and Infrastructure Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
January 30, 2026, by and between Klaus Compliance Solutions, Inc. ("Company") and Hooli Cloud
Services, Inc. ("Vendor"), for the provision of production cloud hosting infrastructure.

Section 1. Definitions

1.1 "Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by either Party in
connection with this Agreement, including Company's system architecture, source code
repositories, and customer data hosted on Vendor's infrastructure.

1.2 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural
person hosted or processed on Vendor's infrastructure on Company's behalf.

1.3 "Security Incident" means any confirmed unauthorized access to, or loss of, Personal Data
or Company's hosted systems.

Section 2. Confidentiality

2.1 Each Party shall protect the other Party's Confidential Information using industry-standard
safeguards and shall not disclose such information except to personnel with a legitimate need
to know who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Section 2.

2.2 Vendor shall not access Company's hosted data except as necessary to provide the Services
or as required to comply with Company's support requests.

2.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations under this Section 2 shall survive termination of
this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, given the long operational lifespan of hosted
production systems and the sensitivity of the data involved.

Section 3. Data Handling and Security

3.1 Vendor maintains a SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure environment and shall provide
Company with a current copy of its SOC 2 report upon request, no more than once per year.

3.2 Vendor shall encrypt all Personal Data at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.2 or
higher, and shall maintain network segmentation between Company's environment and other
customers' environments.

3.3 Breach Notification. Vendor shall notify Company within forty-eight (48) hours of
confirming a Security Incident, and shall provide a preliminary incident report within five (5)
business days, with a full root-cause analysis within thirty (30) days.

Section 4. Limitation of Liability

4.1 Each Party's aggregate liability arising out of this Agreement shall not exceed the greater
of the total fees paid by Company in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or five million
dollars ($5,000,000), whichever is lower.

4.2 The limitation in Section 4.1 does not apply to Vendor's liability for a Security Incident
resulting from Vendor's failure to implement the safeguards required under Section 3.2.

Section 5. Term and Termination

5.1 This Agreement has an initial term of thirty-six (36) months and renews automatically for
successive twelve (12) month terms unless either Party provides at least sixty (60) days' notice
of non-renewal.

5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior
written notice, subject to a data migration assistance period described in Section 5.3.

5.3 Upon termination, Vendor shall provide Company with a ninety (90) day data migration
assistance period, after which Vendor shall securely delete all remaining Company data.

Section 6. General Provisions

6.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Washington.

6.2 Service Level Agreement. Vendor guarantees 99.9% infrastructure uptime, measured monthly,
with service credits available for downtime exceeding this threshold as detailed in Exhibit A.
